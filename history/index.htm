Project Information A brief overview of the project's background, scope, acknowledgements, sources, copyright and dedication.
History Beginning in 1797 with the creation of Nepean Township this history section describes the evolution of Ottawa East up to 1907.
Mainstreeter First printed in October of 1985, this community newspaper includes several historically important articles.
Churches Trinity Anglican (Ascension) was the first followed by Ste. Famille, Wesley United, Calvary Baptist and Canadian Martyrs.
Scholasticate St. Joseph's Scholasticate (now Deschâtelets) is the domain of the Oblates of Mary Immaculate (OMI) - the first and most important institution in the history of Ottawa East.
Schools Beginning in 1875, SS17 later became Lady Evelyn followed by Holy Family/Martyrs, De Mazenod, St. Patrick's College and St. Paul University and others.
Railways The Railroad was the major industry here until the 1950's. This section describes the effect the railway presence had on the community.
Notables Most activities in the Village were controlled by a small group of exceptional people. Brief snapshots of many are included here
After 1907 The section includes several significant events in the evolution of Ottawa East after 1907.
1901 Snapshot Using the 1901 Census, maps, and various records, a descriptive overview of the community is included here.
Air Photo Study Using a treasure trove of unique air photos, the evolution of the community can be seen from 1922 to 1961.
Image Library The central part of this project involves the hundreds of historical photographs gathered from a myriad of sources.
Databases Much can be learned about a community by analysis of important records such as directories, censuses and assessment rolls. Many are reproduced here.
